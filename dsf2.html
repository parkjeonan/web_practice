Dose factors and what they mean

10 to 20

Dose size too small causing poor distribution or cone in the slab. EC control more difficult.

20 to 30

Optimal range for dose size to maximize cone. Aim more for 20 for increasing water content (Period 1) and higher for EC control (Period 3). Flow rates also have an effect. With 4 lts drippers the dose is applied very quickly so a dose factor towards 20 would be better. Where the dose is applied more slowly the dose factor can be higher.

30+

Doze size too large giving good EC control but low water content and dry top to slab.
